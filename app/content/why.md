Rather than send a resume and a cover letter and ask you to imagine what I might build, I wanted to show concretely how I think about data and AI in the context of an investment firm. A document can describe a way of working; a small piece of working software demonstrates it. This app is the latter — a deliberately narrow demonstration rather than a portfolio dump.

The scope is intentionally small. Three working demos and a transcript-analysis placeholder, nothing more. I could have built ten things half-way; I would rather show three things that run end-to-end and one I can talk through honestly. The point is to make it easier for you to evaluate whether the way I ship software is the way you want software shipped at Merewether.

Merewether is taking a thoughtful first step into data science, and the right hire should match that thoughtfulness with restraint and prioritization. The temptation when joining a firm with no prior data-science hire is to arrive with a wishlist of trendy tools and a six-month roadmap. I do not think that is the right move. The right move is to find the workflows that already exist, remove friction from them, and earn the standing to propose larger changes later.

Everything in this app is reproducible from public data sources and a single API key.
