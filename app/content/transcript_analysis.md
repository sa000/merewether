A tool I built in the past was an AI tool to analyze transcripts during earnings season. Following the same clean Data Warehouse concept, we could maintain a database of transcript files so users can pick which transcript to analyze and ask questions directly against it, rather than reading through the entire file.

In principle, this sounds like a useful application, but this is where I'd pause and push back on the team about its actual value. It's good for a sanity check, but the feedback I've gotten, both from analysts and from my PM when implementing the previous version, is that analysts are so deeply immersed during earnings season that they're going to read the transcripts regardless of the tool. A summary helps, but they'll still go through the source material themselves.
A few related ideas could spawn from this tool though:

A quick summarizer or classifier that tags transcripts (for example, even simple as bullish vs. bearish) and lets us run analytics on top of those tags. Layering on sentiment and building a dashboard around it would give the team insights that are difficult to produce by hand.
When asking questions about a transcript, surfacing the specific quotes that support each answer. That alone would be a valuable annotation tool for analysts.

I'd want to gather feedback from the team on how to push these ideas forward, because this is the principle we should be applying. As I mentioned, we can't just throw AI at every problem. We should be more thoughtful about where it will actually make us faster.
